Poland

Poland, a nation with a tumultuous yet resilient history, emerged in the 10th century under Mieszko I and later experienced a "Golden Age" of growth before facing centuries of partitions by neighboring powers. The nation was reborn in 1918 but was again devastated by the Nazi invasion in 1939, marking the start of World War II and leading to millions of Polish deaths and eventual rule under the communist Soviet Union. However, in 1989, the Solidarity movement helped overthrow communism, ushering in a new era of democracy and a dynamic, market-based economy in modern Poland.
